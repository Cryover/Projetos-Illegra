Elvis Presley - Timeline

  • 1935 - Elvis was born into a poor family in Mississippi.

  • 1946 - Elvis' parents bought him a guitar for his 11th birthday. It was love at first sight.

  • 1948 - Elvis's family moved him to Memphis when he was 13 years old.

  • 1953 - Elvis did a short series of recordings with the Memphis Recording Service. In 1954 one of his recordings got good ratings on the radio.

  • 1954 - After doing well on the radio, Elvis and his group performed at a live show. The crowd loved him.

  • 1955 - Elvis got a recording contract with RCA records.

  • 1956 - Elvis headed to Nashville where he recorded his first record. It included one of his hits, Heartbreak Hotel.

  • 1956 - Elvis' TV debut is on a show called "Stage Show".

  • 1956 - Elvis starred in a film with Paramount Pictures called Love Me Tender.

  • 1957 - Elvis bought his now-famous home in Memphis, TN. He named it Graceland.

  • 1958 - Elvis was inducted into the United States Army draft. He showed up for duty with pride.

  • 1960 - Elvis was honorably discharged from the army. He returned home and made a record-breaking album called GI Blues.

  • 1967 - Elvis married a woman named Priscilla. They had met while Elvis was in the army in Germany.

  • 1970 - Elvis met President Richard Nixon at the White House.

  • 1971 - Elvis' Lifetime Achievement Award came from the National Academy of Recording Arts and Sciences.

  • 1977 - Elvis died at his home, Graceland. His legacy would live on for decades to come.

“The first time that I appeared on stage, it scared me to death. I really didn’t know what all the yelling was about. I didn’t realize that my body was moving. It’s a natural thing to me. So to the manager backstage I said, ‘What’d I do? What’d I do?’ And he said “Whatever it is, go back and do it again.”

- From a 1972 taped interview used in MGM’s documentary "Elvis on Tour"
